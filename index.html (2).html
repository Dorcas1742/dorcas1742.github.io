About me

I am not a single-discipline professional.
I never have been.

My background is in Forestry and Wildlife Management — but from the beginning I was drawn to the bigger questions: how do you protect ecosystems when people depend on them? How do you communicate the urgency of climate change to communities that have never heard the word "biodiversity"? How do you make research matter beyond the page it's printed on?

Those questions led me to ICEED, where I work at the intersection of climate research, clean energy advocacy, and policy communication. They led me to found the Green Revolution Initiative, where I bring environmental education directly into schools. And they are leading me toward graduate studies internationally, where I plan to deepen my expertise in environmental health, climate resilience, and ecosystem science.

I am also a spoken word artist, a fashion entrepreneur, and someone who has held more leadership positions than she can count — because I believe that the people working to protect the planet need to lead, communicate, create, and connect, not just analyse.

How I work

From evidence to impact

Every project I touch follows this logic — whether it's a research study, an advocacy campaign, or a community programme.

01
Understand the context
I start with the ecosystem — ecological, social, and political. No solution works without understanding the full system it lives in.
02
Research with rigour
Literature review, field data, stakeholder input, or secondary analysis — whatever the evidence base requires.
03
Communicate to move people
Findings become policy briefs, campaign materials, community conversations, or spoken word — whatever the audience needs to act.
04
Measure and iterate
MEAL frameworks keep me honest. I track what worked, what didn't, and how to do better — on the next project and the next.
